Pastapedia

Penne

Created in 1865 by Giovanni Battista Capurro, from the San Martino d'Albaro Region of Italy.

Conchiglie

Reminiscent of shells, this pasta is pronounced as con-keel-yay and can come in a variety of sizes.

Rigatoni

Tube shaped pasta that comes from the Italian word rigato which means "ridged" or "lined".

Orecchiette

This ear shaped pasta translates to "ear" in Italian and originated from Southern Italy.

Tagliatelle

Legend has it that it was created by a court chef in 1487.

Fusilli

A helical or corkscrew shape that comes from fuso meaning "spindle" in Italian.

Spaghetti

A long, thin, solid pasta that has become one of the most recognized pastas in the world..

Gnocchi

Derived from the Italian word nocchio which means "knot" and is made of semolina and potato.

Farfalle

This type of pasta can come in a variety of sizes and distinctive in it's bow like shape..

Rotelle

A type of pasta that represents a wheel with spokes.

Acini de Pepe

This translates into Seeds of Pepper and is similar to couscous.

Cellentani

This type of pasta is usually scored with lines and ridges.
